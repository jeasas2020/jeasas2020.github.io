The Health Benefits of Almond Butter

Posted in Cholesterol, Diet & Nutrition

Almond butter is packed with endurance-boosting nutrients like healthy fats and protein. Protein helps you prevent muscle wasting during exercise and it also prevents you from feeling too hungry when you do exercise. Almond butter's healthy fats provide you with energy that lasts for hours.

Almond butter is a great complement to things like whole-wheat toast because it creates an energy packed snack that helps fuel you for hours. Almond butter is a wonderful alternative to peanut butter for those who cannot easily tolerate tree nuts. Almonds have many health benefits from regulating blood sugar to preventing gallstones to lowering cholesterol so they are a smart choice for any table.

Ayurveda medicine considers almonds to be one of the most beneficial of all nuts, because almonds are thought to build one's vital essence or life force energy, also known as Chi.

Vitamins and Minerals

Almond butter has significantly more fiber, calcium, and potassium than sunflower seeds or peanut butter. Almond butter is also an excellent source of magnesium, manganese, as well as phosphorous and copper. Almond butter is also an excellent source of vitamin E, and riboflavin.

A serving of almonds has approximately 163 calories and 14 grams of healthy fat. Almonds are great on their own and make a great addition to any dessert, snack or salad because they are very satisfying.
