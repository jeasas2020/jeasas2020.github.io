Extremely Useful Information...You're Welcome!

Congratulations! You've bought some data. Clearly you are now on your way to fulfilling the demands of today's consumer. So...what now?

Data is a relatively new concept, and so once the initial thrill of buying all your lovely new data is over, the realisation that you have no idea what to do next hits many like a ton of bricks.

To avoid this, take a look at our free tips and advice. I'm afraid we haven't included everything - we save all the best stuff for our customers!

How to start using data to your advantage

Step 1: Make sure it's the right data.

You're probably thinking "What? OF COURSE I'll buy the right data!" but it is shocking how often data is selected based on opinion, gut feeling, who companies want to sell to, instead of who actually spends money with them. Analysing your existing customer database will establish who your best-fit customers are and RightMarket (because we're brilliant) will profile this information to pull out more 'good fit' clients for you.

Buying the wrong data means your marketing is no more targeted than it was before, only now you're spending extra money on useless data. Try explaining that one to the boss!

Step 2: Find somewhere to put it.

Reaching the right market and demanding their undivided attention is tough! Particularly today, with advertising seeping its way into each aspect of everyday life; consumers switch off. Marketing is a journey, and integrated marketing is the perfect way to take consumers on that journey, because it repeats and reinforces your message at many different times and during many different activities in consumers' lives - triggering awareness, interest, opinion, perception and action.

Step 3: ‘Tailor, Tailor, Tailor.’ Should be ‘Tailor, Tailor, TAILOR.’

One more time? TAILOR. When it comes to content it can be difficult to establish what's useful and what's useless. As marketers we want every piece of 'valuable' information on everything we produce; and while we may think our content is brilliant, amazing, a stroke of genius! (if we do say so ourselves), in reality consumers skim over most things they consider to be advertising. The trick is to get their attention instantly, and convince them that they NEED to hear what you have to say.

Why Would David, a young family man, want to hear about your new 2 seat sports car? Equally, why would Harry, a young bachelor, be interested in a deal on your most reliable people carrier?

Personalisation

Personalisationis an incredibly powerful marketing tool - in 2011 alone it generated 31.4% EXTRA revenue for those who used it! Not convinced? Here are some more great stats you can't ignore:

  • A Marketing Sherpa test showed personalised email achieved a 137% higher open rate and a 129% higher clickthrough rate
  • 42% of people are more likely to read a direct mail piece if it addresses their unique needs
  • BMW generated $500,000 in revenue by personalising MMS messages to 1,200 customers in the US, improving conversions by 30%
  • Personalising marketing materials can increase response rates by 36%,
  • Personalising marketing materials can increase response time 34%
  • Personalising marketing materials can increase repeat orders by 46%.
  • Personalising marketing materials can Leading to an overall increase in profit and revenue of 30%!
